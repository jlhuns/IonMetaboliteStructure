CC   -!- FUNCTION: Plays an essential role in the initiation and regulation of
CC       chromosomal replication. ATP-DnaA binds to the origin of replication
CC       (oriC) to initiate formation of the DNA replication initiation complex
CC       once per cell cycle. Binds the DnaA box (a 9 base pair repeat at the
CC       origin) and separates the double-stranded (ds)DNA. Forms a right-handed
CC       helical filament on oriC DNA; dsDNA binds to the exterior of the
CC       filament while single-stranded (ss)DNA is stabiized in the filament's
CC       interior. The ATP-DnaA-oriC complex binds and stabilizes one strand of
CC       the AT-rich DNA unwinding element (DUE), permitting loading of DNA
CC       polymerase. After initiation quickly degrades to an ADP-DnaA complex
CC       that is not apt for DNA replication. Binds acidic phospholipids.
CC       {ECO:0000255|HAMAP-Rule:MF_00377}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Oligomerizes as a right-handed, spiral filament on DNA at
CC       oriC. {ECO:0000255|HAMAP-Rule:MF_00377}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cytoplasm {ECO:0000255|HAMAP-Rule:MF_00377}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: Domain I is involved in oligomerization and binding regulators,
CC       domain II is flexibile and of varying length in different bacteria,
CC       domain III forms the AAA+ region, while domain IV binds dsDNA.
CC       {ECO:0000255|HAMAP-Rule:MF_00377}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the DnaA family. {ECO:0000255|HAMAP-
CC       Rule:MF_00377}.

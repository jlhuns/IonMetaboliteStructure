CC   -!- FUNCTION: Flagellin is the subunit protein which polymerizes to form
CC       the filaments of bacterial flagella. FlaA binds to flagellar assembly
CC       factor FliW protein, preventing FliW from binding to CsrA, so that CsrA
CC       can then bind flaA mRNA and represses its translation.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:27229370}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Heteromer of FlaA and FlaB. Interacts with FliW.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:27229370}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Secreted. Bacterial flagellum.
CC   -!- DISRUPTION PHENOTYPE: Has 2 short flagella probably composed of
CC       flagellin B (flaB). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:27229370}.
CC   -!- MISCELLANEOUS: The mRNA is localized to the poles of short, probably
CC       elongating, cells; mRNA localization is lost in an fliW deletion strain
CC       but restored in a csrA-fliW double deletion. Correct localization
CC       requires protein translation; mutation of the start codon to prevent
CC       translation initiation or a premature stop codon (at position 3)
CC       prevents mRNA localization, a stop codon at position 101 leads to
CC       partial localization to the cell poles. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:27229370}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the bacterial flagellin family. {ECO:0000305}.
